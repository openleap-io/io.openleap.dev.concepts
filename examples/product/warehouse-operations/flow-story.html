Warehouse Operations

Product Artifact Flow Story — how 59 artifacts connect from business need to platform resolution

1. It Starts With a Problem

1
Project Scope — Why does this product exist?

A mid-size manufacturer operates a 5,000 m² warehouse with 12 employees. Goods receipt is paper-based, bin assignments live in Excel, pick lists are printed daily. The result: 3–5% of stock is "lost" in wrong bins, inventory data is always 24 hours stale, and pick errors average 2.1% per week.

Project Scope

5 goals defined — from "eliminate paper-based goods receipt" (must-have) to "warehouse utilization dashboard" (should-have). Constraints: Zebra TC52 scanners, 06:00–22:00 operating hours, ISO 9001 traceability.

↓
Scope defines boundaries → determines which processes and capabilities are in/out

Business Events — What triggers what?

+
Also produced during discovery

These artifacts provide additional context:

  • Glossary Terms (Material, Goods Receipt, Bin Location, ...) → feed into Suite UBL
  • Quality Requirements (scan latency < 1s, 99.5% availability) → become service NFRs
  • External Interfaces (barcode scanner, ERP procurement/sales, logistics API) → define integration boundaries
  • KPIs (receipt time < 30min, pick accuracy > 99.5%) → measure process success
  • Decisions (separate inbound/outbound, mobile-first UI) → trace to suite ADRs
  • Workflow Candidates (nightly inventory recon, auto-replenishment) → become Telos Workflow Specs
